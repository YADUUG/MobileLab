Introduction

Neurological disorders are diseases of the central and peripheral nervous system, affecting the brain, spinal cord, nerves, nerve roots, autonomic nervous system, and muscles. These disorders include:

  • Epilepsy
  • Alzheimer’s disease and other dementias
  • Cerebrovascular diseases including stroke
  • Migraine and other headache disorders
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Parkinson’s disease
  • Neuroinfections
  • Brain tumors
  • Traumatic disorders of the nervous system due to head trauma
  • Neurological disorders as a result of malnutrition
"Neurological disorders are diseases of the central and peripheral nervous system." - WHO

Existing Solutions

Currently, neurological examinations involve a range of manual and observational techniques conducted by trained medical professionals. Common methods include assessing:

  • Reflexes
  • Muscle strength
  • Coordination
  • Cognitive function

However, these traditional methods can be time-consuming, expensive, and may not always provide real-time insights into a patient's neurological condition.

Our Solution: NeuraBlink

Using computer vision and AI technology, particularly the Mediapipe library, NeuraBlink monitors and analyzes hand movements in real-time. The system detects and records hand movements, such as finger taps, and provides relevant data for neurological examination tests.

The process involves:

  • Capturing video input
  • Processing it using AI models to recognize hand landmarks
  • Interpreting the movements to gather information about a user's motor skills

The collected data, including tap counts, speed, and distances, can be valuable for neurological assessments.

Benefits

NeuraBlink serves as an efficient platform for diagnosing various neurological disorders. Unlike traditional methods that require writing for diagnosis, NeuraBlink is beneficial for individuals who are not capable of reading and writing.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, many patients suffered due to the lack of proper diagnosis. NeuraBlink can serve as a contact-free detection tool for any future pandemics, with minor modifications.

In practical scenarios, individuals from villages often travel to nearby metro cities for expert opinions from MD doctors. This diagnosis tool helps doctors accurately analyze many patients in a limited time frame, providing a complete pipeline for correct diagnosis, including finger taps, hand flip movements, eye movements, and more.

Neurological Test Detection Modules

Finger Tap Detection

Description: This module evaluates fine motor skills by analyzing finger tap movements.

Features: Tracks the number of taps, speed, and precision of finger movements.

Benefits: Provides detailed insights into motor function, aiding in the diagnosis of disorders such as Parkinson's disease and other motor neuron diseases.

Noise Detection

Description: This module assesses auditory processing and reaction to auditory stimuli.

Features: Measures response time and accuracy to various auditory signals.

Benefits: Helps in diagnosing auditory processing disorders and can be used to evaluate the effectiveness of treatments and therapies.

Hand Flip Detection

Description: This module monitors hand flip movements to evaluate coordination and dexterity.

Features: Analyzes the speed and consistency of hand flip motions.

Benefits: Useful for assessing conditions that affect motor coordination, such as multiple sclerosis and other neurological disorders.

How to Use the Models

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Shoot a video (in MP4 format) of the patient performing the test. Ensure the movements are clearly visible to the camera frame.
  2. Click the "Browser files" button to upload the MP4 video for analysis.
  3. After upload the video file, please wait till analysis is completed. **Option to download the CSV file**
